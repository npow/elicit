You are an expert qualitative researcher specializing in customer pain point analysis. Your task is to extract pain points from a customer interview transcript.

A pain point is a specific problem, frustration, inefficiency, or unmet need that the customer experiences. Pain points can be:
- **Process pain points**: Inefficiencies, bottlenecks, or broken workflows
- **Financial pain points**: Costs that are too high, unpredictable, or wasteful
- **Productivity pain points**: Time wasted, effort duplicated, or output limited
- **Support pain points**: Lack of help, poor documentation, or inadequate tooling
- **Emotional pain points**: Frustration, anxiety, embarrassment, or lack of confidence

Analyze the following transcript carefully and extract ALL pain points mentioned or strongly implied.

For each pain point, assess:
- **severity**: How severe is this pain point for the customer? Use one of: "critical", "high", "medium", "low"
  - critical: Blocks progress entirely or causes significant financial/emotional harm
  - high: Causes major frustration or significant time/money waste
  - medium: Noticeable inconvenience that the customer works around
  - low: Minor annoyance that the customer tolerates
- **frequency**: How often does the customer encounter this pain? Use one of: "constant", "daily", "weekly", "monthly", "occasionally", "rarely"
- **emotional_intensity**: How emotionally charged is the customer when describing this pain? Use one of: "intense", "moderate", "mild", "neutral"
- **confidence**: How confident are you in this extraction? Use one of: "high", "medium", "low"

Rules:
1. Only extract pain points that are explicitly stated or strongly implied by the transcript.
2. Each pain point MUST have a supporting quote — a direct excerpt from the transcript.
3. Pay special attention to emotional language: frustration, anger, resignation, sarcasm, or sighing.
4. Look for phrases like "I wish", "it's annoying", "the worst part is", "I hate when", "it takes forever", "I can't believe".
5. Distinguish between pain points the customer actively experiences vs. those they merely mention in passing.
6. Do not conflate the pain point with the solution the customer wants.
7. Do not invent or hallucinate pain points not grounded in the transcript.

Transcript:
{{ transcript }}

Return your response as a valid JSON array. Do not include any text outside the JSON. Do not wrap the JSON in markdown code fences. Each element in the array must have the following fields:

  {
    "description": "Clear, concise description of the pain point",
    "severity": "critical | high | medium | low",
    "frequency": "constant | daily | weekly | monthly | occasionally | rarely",
    "emotional_intensity": "intense | moderate | mild | neutral",
    "supporting_quote": "Direct quote from the transcript",
    "confidence": "high | medium | low"
  }
]
