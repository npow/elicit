Interviewer: What led you to start looking for a new mattress?
Interviewee: Ongoing poor sleep. I was waking up tired, irritable, and less patient at work and at home. I kept saying I needed to fix it, but put it off because the decision felt expensive and hard.

Interviewer: Why hard?
Interviewee: I couldn't tell quality from marketing claims. In stores, every bed felt different for five minutes, but I needed confidence about months of sleep. The downside of choosing wrong felt huge.

Interviewer: What did you try before buying?
Interviewee: Rotating the old mattress, changing pillows, adjusting bedtime habits. Those were temporary patches. I still woke up sore and restless.

Interviewer: What finally pushed you forward?
Interviewee: A streak of bad nights during a high-stress work period. I realized sleep quality was directly hurting decisions and relationships.

Interviewer: How did you evaluate alternatives?
Interviewee: Reviews, return policies, and conversations with people I trust. Trial period and return friction became more important than feature claims.

Interviewer: What was the biggest fear?
Interviewee: Spending a lot and ending up with another bad fit, then dealing with painful returns.

Interviewer: What would make this purchase easier?
Interviewee: A guided path that narrows choices fast, explains tradeoffs simply, and makes the risk reversal obvious.
