Interviewer: Walk me through the moment you decided to replace your phone.
Interviewee: I had been using a basic prepaid phone. It technically worked, but I felt left behind. Friends were doing more with maps, photos, and apps, and I had to borrow devices or wait until I got home.

Interviewer: Why didn't you switch earlier?
Interviewee: The contract commitment felt risky. I was okay paying low monthly costs and didn't want to be locked in for years. Going to a store also felt like walking into a sales funnel. I expected pressure and didn't trust I'd get neutral advice.

Interviewer: What changed?
Interviewee: A few moments stacked up: I missed directions while traveling, couldn't quickly share pictures, and felt inefficient at work. I started thinking the hidden cost of keeping my old phone was bigger than the bill savings.

Interviewer: How did you evaluate options?
Interviewee: Mostly online research, forums, and asking friends to show me their phones in real situations. I compared plans in spreadsheets because carrier sites made it hard to understand total cost over time.

Interviewer: What was most frustrating in the process?
Interviewee: Unclear tradeoffs. Every option looked good in marketing language, but I wanted to know real downside risk: battery life over time, contract penalties, and whether I'd regret overpaying.

Interviewer: What was the final trigger to buy?
Interviewee: A travel day where my old phone failed at navigation and coordination. I decided the stress was no longer worth it.

Interviewer: What made the purchase feel safe enough?
Interviewee: Seeing transparent plan math and hearing from trusted peers. If I had gotten that clarity earlier, I would've upgraded sooner.
